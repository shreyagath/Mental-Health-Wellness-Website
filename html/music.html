Piano

The piano is an acoustic, stringed musical instrument invented in Italy by Bartolomeo Cristofori around the year 1700 in which the strings are struck by wooden hammers that are coated with a softer material. It is played using a keyboard, which is a row of keys (small levers) that the performer presses down or strikes with the fingers and thumbs of both hands to cause the hammers to strike the strings.

Drums

Playing the drums is probably one of the most active ways to play music. Naturally it will keep your heart-rate up and provide you with some cardio. It is also a very cathartic instrument. Playing any instrument is therapeutic, now imagine doing that while getting to hit stuff! Learning drums can boost academic performance especially in math; after all, drummers have to count! Drumming can also help students learn fractions, frequencies and intervals.

Check out the basics of drums and get started with the hitting.









Guitar

Playing the guitar will help your body and mind with immense benefits. Studies show that playing the guitar improves the grey matter in the brain resulting in improved memory power. Additionally, there is less decline in memory power with age. Playing the guitar demands your complete attention and gives you an escape from the world.

Check out the basics of Guitar and get started by learning it.









Violin

it has come to observation in adults that there are, Lower levels of depression, anxiety, and other stress related health problems. Playing the violin is an excellent stress reliever and a great way to enlarge your social circle. Today's sedentary lifestyle leaves many adults with unhealthy spines and terrible, pain-inducing posture. Playing the violin calls for exemplary posture. Learning how to stand properly while playing can translate to improved posture all the time.
